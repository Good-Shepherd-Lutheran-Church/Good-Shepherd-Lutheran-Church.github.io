Uniforms

GSLS believes that the way the child feels about himself and the way they dress directly influences their attitude about school. Since school can be thought of as the child’s workplace, the way he or she dresses should reflect this philosophy. The School Board has adopted an official school uniform. All students in grades K-5 will wear their uniforms except on special days that will be specified. Our school uniform offers students several choices in the form of colors and styles of clothing, while providing consistency, neatness, safety and a sense of modesty. We want to dress our best out of respect for our Lord.

Attire

The school uniform attire consists of a polo shirt, slacks, shorts, skorts, jumpers, dresses, sweatshirts or cardigans, which can be ordered through the school. If purchasing outside of GSLS, these items should be khaki or navy. A white blouse or shirt may be worn. Socks or tights must be worn in solid colors; white, khaki, or navy. Leather shoes or athletic style sneakers are acceptable. The school requires the purchase of at least one polo shirt with the school’s logo on it from the school.

Rules

  1. All shorts, jumpers, skorts, and skirts must be worn at an appropriate length. Avoid sagging or tight-fitting uniforms.

  2. All students may wear shorts year-round.

  3. To maintain modesty, girls should wear shorts under skirts, jumpers, and dresses.

  4. Make-up of any kind is prohibited.

  5. For health and safety reasons, all students must wear socks and closed toe shoes. Athletic or dress shoes are acceptable. Sandal-style shoes and flip flops may not be worn.

  6. Hairstyles should be clean, well-groomed, and out of the eyes. Hair length should not interfere with physical activities or be a distraction in the classroom.

  7. On PE days, athletic shoes/gym shoes should be worn.

We expect both students and parents to adhere to and support these guidelines. Any child not in compliance with these guidelines will be asked to correct the infraction and a note will be sent to the parent explaining the problem. The administrative personnel reserve the right to have a child call home for proper clothing when out of dress code.
